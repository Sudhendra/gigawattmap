# Gigawatt Map v0.1 — Launch Notes

**Date:** 2026-04-26
**Tag:** `v0.1.0`

> AI capex is constrained by the grid, not by chips. Capital is moving faster
> than transmission planning. Most of the people allocating that capital can't
> see where the constraints are. v0.1 is the first cut at fixing that.

---

## What shipped

A single public map (`gigawattmap.com`) that overlays every known AI
datacenter on Earth with the power plants, transmission proxies, submarine
cables, and community opposition that bound the buildout.

`/v1/manifest.json` and versioned alongside the artifacts in R2.

### Application surface

- **Map** (`/`) — MapLibre + deck.gl, four toggleable layers, click-to-drill
  intelligence drawer with shareable canonical URLs (`?lat=…&lng=…&zoom=…`).
- **Ticker** (`/api/v1/tickers`) — live capacity / queue indicators along the
  bottom rail, refreshed at the edge.
- **Search** (R2-backed `search-index.json`) — instant fuzzy search over
  campuses, operators, and metro areas.
- **Share** — per-view canonical URL + dynamic OG image
  (`/api/v1/og?lat=…&lng=…&zoom=…&title=…`).
- **Methodology** (`/about`) — every source, every license, every known gap,
  versioned with `LAST_UPDATED`.
- **Data downloads** (`/data`, `/data/api`) — direct R2 URLs per source,
  attribution and license terms inline.
- **404, sitemap, robots, dynamic favicon** — see commit `2256276`.

### Stack

- **Web:** Next.js 15 (App Router), Tailwind v4, MapLibre GL, deck.gl,
  Protomaps + PMTiles
- **API:** Cloudflare Workers + R2 + Pages
- **Pipeline:** Python 3.11, `uv`, `pydantic` v2, `geopandas`, `httpx`, `typer`
- **Tests:** vitest (108 passing), Playwright e2e on critical paths
- **Repo:** pnpm monorepo (`apps/web`, `apps/api`, `packages/types`,
  `packages/ui`, `data-pipeline/`)

## What didn't ship (intentional)

- **Account system / saved views.** v0.1 is read-only and stateless; URL is
  the only persistence layer.
- **Real-time queue data feeds.** ERCOT / PJM / MISO interconnect queues are
  on the roadmap but each requires a per-ISO scraper and a normalization
  pass; punted to v0.2.
- **Transmission line geometry.** Drawing every kV line at zoom 4 kills the
  60fps budget. Probably ships as a heatmap, not lines.
- **Water-stress overlay.** WRI Aqueduct integration is scoped but the
  per-basin attribution work is not done. v0.2.
- **Embeddable map widget.** Asked for by two pre-launch readers; the auth
  + rate limit story for embeds is non-trivial. v0.2.
- **Mobile-first layout.** The map is usable on mobile but the intelligence
  drawer is desktop-tuned. Acceptable for a launch where the audience is at
  a laptop.

## Known gaps (mirrored from `/about`)

- **China.** OSM tagging is sparse in the mainland and regulatory filings are
  not public. Best-effort from press coverage and operator disclosures.
- **India.** Improving as hyperscaler builds are announced, but lags US/EU.
- **Russia, Belarus, Central Asia.** Poor. We would rather show gaps than
  guess.
- **Pre-2020 colo MW.** Frequently estimated from footprint, not disclosed.
  Treat as ranges.
- **Neoclouds.** Lease and announce faster than our refresh cadence. If you
  see a fresh announcement, it probably beats the map.

## What's next (v0.2 candidates)

In priority order, subject to operator triage:

1. **ISO interconnect queues** (ERCOT first; it's the binding constraint that
   most people quote and the one with the cleanest public data)
2. **Transmission heatmap** (HIFLD / OpenInfraMap as a starting point)
3. **Water stress overlay** (WRI Aqueduct, basin-level)
4. **Embeddable widget** with rate-limited public token
5. **Dataset diff feed** — when a new datacenter or power plant lands in the
   pipeline, push to a public RSS / webhook so analysts can subscribe
6. **Per-campus history panel** — every announcement / permit / opposition
   filing on a single timeline

Roadmap is a living artifact; treat this list as a snapshot, not a contract.
